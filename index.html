💡 How This Works

In Canada, couples file taxes separately — but some deductions work better on one return than the other.

For example, medical expenses and childcare usually save more when claimed by the lower-income spouse. Charitable donations are better combined on the higher-income spouse for a bigger credit.

📌 Tips Everyone Should Know
💰
Basic Personal Amount — always automatic
Every Canadian gets $16,129 federal + $22,323 Alberta tax-free. Wealthsimple applies this automatically.
🏦
RRSP = instant tax savings
Every $1 in an RRSP reduces your taxable income by $1. A $5,000 contribution can save you $700–$1,000+ depending on your bracket. Deadline: March 2, 2026.
🏠
Work from home? Claim it
If your employer signed a T2200, you can deduct part of your rent, internet, and utilities. Ask your HR department.
🩺
Medical expenses add up
Prescriptions, dental, glasses, physio all count. Claim everything above $2,635 (or 3% of net income). Best claimed on the lower-income spouse.
❤️
Bundle charitable donations
First $200 = 15% credit. Everything above = 29% credit. Combine 2 years of donations into one return for a bigger payout. Claim on the higher earner.
📋
Check Box 44 on your T4
This shows union dues already paid. Wealthsimple claims it automatically. Also check any professional membership fees you paid yourself.
👶
Childcare goes on the lower earner
Daycare, summer camp, babysitters. Must be claimed by the lower-income spouse. Up to $8,000/child under 7 and $5,000 age 7–16.
⚡
Set up direct deposit
Register through CRA My Account or your bank. Refund arrives in 2 weeks instead of 6–8 weeks by cheque.
⚠️
File on time even if you can't pay
Late filing when you owe = 5% penalty + 1% per month. File on time, pay what you can. Always.
